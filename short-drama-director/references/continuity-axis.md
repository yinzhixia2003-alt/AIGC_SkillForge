# Continuity and Axis

Use this reference for dialogue, confrontation, pursuit, entrances, corridors, roads, crowds, and any scene spanning multiple shots.

## Internal ledger

Track active on/off-screen people and threats; foreground/middle/background and left/center/right positions; facing, eyeline, and movement; prop hand, owner, quantity and state; doors, vehicles, routes, damage, debris, weather, light; main axis and camera side.

The prior shot's end state is the next continuous shot's factual start. Do not silently move people, swap a prop, close a door, repair damage, disperse a crowd, or reverse direction.

## Axis setup

Choose the dominant line: A-to-B dialogue, opposing groups, authority-to-subject, travel direction, spatial depth, or observer-to-target POV. Stay on one side by default and preserve opposing eyelines.

Change the axis only through a neutral re-establishing shot, visible character repositioning, camera crossing, POV bridge, foreground occlusion, or a new scene/time reset.

## Action continuity

Across a cut, preserve the same hand/foot, direction, speed trend, contact point, progress, and visible result. Never repeat the action start.

## Crowd and threat layering

New people or threats join the current layout; they do not replace active earlier people. In close-ups, retain pressure through frame edge, background, off-screen sound, or the next spatial re-anchor.

## Scene changes

New scenes reset physical continuity but may carry source-supported state such as a held prop, injury, wetness, decision, or emotional consequence. Do not carry old-room sounds, furniture, screen direction, or light into an unrelated space.
