# Selective Transition Design

Use this reference only after the real shots and their cut boundaries exist. The default is an ordinary hard cut with no appendix entry.

## Selection test

Design a transition only when all three are true:

1. The outgoing shot provides a visible or audible exit anchor.
2. The incoming shot provides a compatible source-supported entry anchor.
3. The transition improves time, space, memory, causality, suspense, or emotional comprehension without revealing information early.

If any answer is no, keep the ordinary cut. Never add a transition merely for polish or variety.

When a designed transition is justified, test the least ornamental source-supported candidate in this order: action continuity, sound lead/tail, eyeline, prop/state, graphic/shape/direction match, emotional contrast, then explicit hard cut. This is not a requirement to soften every boundary; an unrelated or intentionally ruptured segment should cut directly.

## Allowed families

- Eyeline match: observer's established gaze -> target reveal.
- Action match: source-supported movement exits -> compatible movement enters.
- Sound lead or sound tail: a supplied sound motivates attention or completes the prior action; do not carry unrelated room tone into a new scene.
- Prop/state match: the same supplied prop or persistent state connects time or space.
- Graphic/shape match: only when both approved compositions already provide the shapes; do not invent objects or metaphorical imagery.
- Occlusion cut: only when a source-present person or object naturally covers the frame.
- Memory entry/return: enter through a physical trigger and return through a supplied physical anchor.
- Black cut, flash, dissolve, or contrast cut: only when the script or director's time/emotional logic supports it.

Do not use a whip pan, motion blur, light flash, smoke, passing object, or foreground wipe unless the required movement/object already exists in the approved shots.

## Boundary discipline

- A transition is a relationship between two real shots, not an extra shot.
- Preserve the outgoing shot's approved end state and the incoming shot's approved start state.
- Do not force spatial continuity across a new scene or intentional rupture.
- Do not use the transition to hide missing story information, reverse an axis, teleport a prop, or repair an overloaded shot.
- At flashback boundaries, name the trigger and the return anchor explicitly.

Use this optional appendix after the primary storyboard and before an SDP handoff appendix:

```markdown
## 选择性转场设计

| 转场位置 | 类型 | 出点 | 入点 | 动机 | 执行说明 | 禁止 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 04→05 | 眼神触发硬切 | 林岚视线锁向右上画外 | 屋檐异物首次出现 | 回答上一镜观看需要 | 视线落点稳定后直接切入目标；不叠化 | 不提前露出异物，不新增闪光 |
```

`转场位置` must contain two adjacent real shot numbers. List only designed transitions; omit ordinary cuts.
