# Platform and Risk Rules

## Platform adaptation

Use only user-supplied or verified platform capabilities: duration, aspect ratio, resolution, first/last-frame support, reference inputs, audio/lip-sync, multi-shot support, syntax, and prompt limit. Without platform information, emit generic natural language.

Do not invent focal length, seed, camera code, weight syntax, or unsupported asset tags.

## Directly repairable expression issues

Without changing directing, you may:

- convert abstract emotion into visible performance;
- clarify camera start/direction/stop;
- state prop count, hand, and position;
- restate an already-established start state;
- keep a phone caller off-screen;
- make unspecified UI unreadable;
- lower background action priority;
- add shot-specific negative constraints.

## Return to director

Return a shot instead of generating a false prompt when:

- dialogue cannot fit approved time;
- multiple independent main actions compete in a short shot;
- POV also demands the observer's full frontal appearance;
- shot size cannot reveal the directed clue/action;
- storyboard and directed blocking are irreconcilable;
- prop state/owner cannot match adjacent shots;
- entry/exit direction or axis is contradictory;
- the approved long take exceeds platform capability and splitting is unauthorized;
- timecodes overlap/gap or shot counts disagree;
- executing the shot would change the plot result.

## High-risk shots

- Phones/screens: lock device count, hand, phone state, caller visibility, and UI readability.
- Multi-person dialogue: lock current speaker/reaction subject, axis, eyelines, and background priority.
- Crowds: keep natural varied motion; do not synchronize or clone the lead.
- Effects: use only directed location, color, path, intensity change, consequence, and end state.
- Injury/fatigue/dirt: only when approved; persist without automatic recovery or exaggeration.
- Action: preserve space, preparation, contact/result, consequence, re-anchor, and direction.
