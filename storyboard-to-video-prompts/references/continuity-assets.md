# Continuity, Assets, and Storyboard Frames

## Asset roles

- Character assets lock identity and approved appearance/state; they do not override directed action or blocking.
- Location assets lock spatial structure, fixed anchors, routes, doors/windows, large objects, time/weather, and main light direction.
- Prop assets lock shape, material, color, quantity, owner, and current state. Momentary hand/location comes from continuity.
- Storyboard references lock composition, position, facing, eyeline, depth, and key states; they cannot add story facts.

Do not invent asset labels. List absent asset classes as `无` or a concise placeholder.

## A/B/C frames

- A: stable shot start.
- B: shot end or principal result.
- C: necessary intermediate contact/turn that A/B cannot imply.

A/B/C remain inside one real video shot. On first/last-frame platforms use A and B as references; otherwise translate them into ordered natural language.

## Continuity ledger

Track visible and off-screen people, depth/screen position, facing, gaze, movement, expression/physiology, prop hand/quantity/state, doors/windows/vehicles/furniture, damage/debris/weather/light, axis side, and sound/threat direction.

Apply full end-to-start matching only to continuous relationships. For state-only cuts, carry necessary persistent facts but not old composition or sound. For new scenes/hard cuts, carry only source-confirmed cross-scene state.

## Close-ups and crowds

An object or face close-up does not erase a continuous surrounding scene. Preserve active pressure through frame edge, background, off-screen sound, or a later re-anchor. New people join rather than replace existing active people.

## Conflict handling

If the storyboard composition, asset spatial structure, or prop continuity cannot all be true, return a specific conflict. Do not move a fixed door, swap hands, duplicate a prop, or change shot intent to hide the mismatch.
